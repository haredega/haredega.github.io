# Distribution Networks and SmartGrids 

Right now I'm in working as a full time Project Engineer within the Decentralised Energy Grid Connection Agency for the Western France region  at [Enedis](http://www.enedis.fr/) ( for all the french lovers: Agence de Raccordement des Grands Producteurs - France Ouest).

In the Agency, we're responsible of accompaigning project owners from their initial research (pre-studies) up to their final solution - when a technical and financial proposition of grid connection is offered, and afterwards until the construction, commissioning phase and the actual grid connection. 

In the engineering office, we're accountable for the network studies, so that each project owner has the viable solution with the least impact on the grid, and, therefore, the best price.

As a project engineer I'm in charge of:
- Network studies
- Financial and technical report writing
- Team performance and workload monitoring

As my close relationship with the distribution grid goes back to college, I choose to do my first 6 month internship at Enedis, in a project for the specification of a web based app. I have also participated in a way more mathematical project in my second internship, this time in Brazil, at [Elektro, an Iberdrola company](https://www.elektro.com.br/) which is of course the concessionary company for the distribution network in my hometown region. It was a project in operational research, with linear programming for decision making. It was during my internship at Elektro, until July 2016.

# Start up, Entrepreneurship and Fintechs

## Pagar.me
I'm my last job as a Risk Management Analyst at [Pagar.me](pagar.me), a PSP (Payment Service Provider) I had an end-to-end participation in operational risk detection and mitigation projects. We've also started to implement a Compliance structure as an answer to the upcoming regulatory challenges on the company. 
It has been a huge opportunity to better understand how working in a financial environment doesn't mean everyone is mean and life's awful and you get no time for yourself, and all of that financial market stuff we hear everywhere.
