Well, first things first,
---
I should say why on earth I'm doing it.
I've been working now for 5 months at [Pagar.me](pagar.me), a Payment Service Provider (PSP) in Brazil.

It is not really a startup anymore, but the ambiance is pretty much the same. We've got
fruit in the office every morning, top notch platforms available and we are all responsible
and accountable of a lot of things, not necessarily related - either to what are our main
duties and also to what were our field of studies.

I've had a great time so far, and even though I didn't have the specific knowledge on
credit card fraud, over time I've found a way to connect the technology and soft skills toolset
I developed on college to my everyday work.

What with this webinar stuff?
---
Well, as I became some sort of *'fraud specialist'* if that exists, I ended up being chosen
 to participate on a webinar with one of our partners, [DotStore](http://site.dotstore.com.br/loja-virtual/), about
 e-commerce fraud. If you happen to speak portuguese, it is  available [right here](insert address).

The feedback we've got so far has been really great, and the ones who joined us have been really nice and made such considerate questions. Even if the subject is one of the least spoken of when you're starting your business it ends up being one of the reasons that makes e-commerces shut.

I'll allow myself to point out some of the key points just to make this post useful, but you really should take a look at the whole presentation, I took the time to go through all of it in detail.

- Why an antifraud system is not enough
- The basic developper toolkit for website protection *and how to make good use of it*
- Preventing chargebacks *tip: it's not all about that toolkit. Logistics and communication are also essential.*
- Getting your money back from those bastards *or: chargeback representation*
- Identifying fraud patterns on your business
- Ways your PSP can help your e-commerce thrive or flop with fraud

That's just the outline, the content is almost an hour of webinar with some really good questions in the end.
