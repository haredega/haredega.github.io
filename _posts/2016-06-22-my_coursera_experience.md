Last year I had the chance to work with Professor [Paulo Lemos](https://www.linkedin.com/in/paulolemos) as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant for the course *Entrepreneurship and Entrepreneurial Competences* [O Empreendedorismo e as Competências do Empreendedor](https://www.coursera.org/learn/empreendedorismo), featured on [Valor](http://www.valor.com.br/carreira/4036998/coursera-lanca-cursos-line-gratuitos-de-instituicoes-brasileiras) and [Exame](http://exame.abril.com.br/tecnologia/noticias/os-10-cursos-gratuitos-mais-populares-no-coursera-em-2015).

With three other teaching assistants, we were in charge of uploading and checking the course videos, making subtitles for the audio lectures and preparing the tests for the platform. Professor Lemos not only made room enough for us to communicate and make remarks, but also kept asking us about different matters that would have a real impact on the course - such as grading policy and how to present the questions so that students would remain engaged throughout the whole course.

I was also requested to keep in touch with the help desk for our questions, and to talk to our partnership manager [Giovanni Dubois](https://www.linkedin.com/in/giovannidubois) during the platform change. It was not so hard as our issues were mostly resolved within a 24h delay. As Professor Lemos was concerned about losing some of the enrolled students during the change of platforms, we made a Skype call to clarify some key points, and there was also a Hangout to present the On-Demand course platform.

This whole experience was very rewarding, and I learned a lot from getting to know the other side of the MOOC universe.
